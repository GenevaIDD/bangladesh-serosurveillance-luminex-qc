Excluded Analytes (soft-flag — kept in data, muted in report)

These bead regions were identified as added in error and should not influence laboratory decisions. They remain in tables and plots but are visually muted and listed in a banner on the report.

Well Classification

Background and NC are distinct concepts: Background wells are the plate blanks (reported alongside the curve); NC wells are a named seronegative sample that gets its own QC section. Anything not matching any pattern is treated as a specimen.

Standard Curve

Default is the Uvira 10-point 4-fold series (62.5 → 16,384,000). Standard1 maps to the first value, Standard2 to the second, etc.

QC Thresholds

The problem-fraction threshold is the cutoff used in the bead-count and range summary cards: an antigen is flagged when at least this fraction of its wells (resp. samples) is problematic. Same number is applied to bead-count problems and to BELOW_RANGE / ABOVE_RANGE counts.

If a standard curve fails QC, try removing each point one at a time (leave-one-out). If dropping a single point produces a passing fit, use that fit and flag the dropped point. Requires at least 6 remaining points.
