Positive Control QC
How Positive Control QC works. Single-point positive controls (PCs) are samples of known antibody status used to track inter-plate assay performance over time. Unlike the standards (a serial-dilution series fit to a 4PL curve), these are loaded at a single nominal level — here the Cholera High and Cholera Low controls, each run in duplicate wells per plate and not fit to a curve. For any given antigen a control should read at a roughly consistent MFI across runs; the two controls are tracked separately. The plot, hover, and table use the same conventions as Background QC.
  • Cross-plate overview (per control). x = antigen (panel order), y = MFI (log scale). Once ≥ 3 past plates exist, a grey bar spans the historical IQR (Q1–Q3) of each antigen's per-plate mean (hover the bar for Q1 / median / Q3); this plate's mean (of its duplicate wells) is a dot — blue within the IQR, orange ♦ above/below it (provisional flag). With < 3 past plates each past plate's mean is a grey dot and this plate's mean is blue. Hover a current dot to read its individual well MFIs.
  • Per-control stats table (collapsed). One row per antigen: n wells; one column per well (this plate's MFI); current Mean / SD / %CV / IQR across the wells; Historical mean / SD / %CV / IQR across each past plate's mean; and n past plates. SD uses n−1; %CV = SD ÷ mean × 100; IQR = Q1–Q3. Rows where this plate's mean falls outside the historical IQR are highlighted — ▲ orange above Q3, ▼ blue below Q1.

Negative Control QC
How Negative Control QC works. A negative control (NC) is a well of pooled seronegative serum (here, pre-2019 North American plasma) that should read antibody-negative for every antigen. Each NC control (e.g. Negative 0 and Negative 49) is tracked separately across plates. The plot, hover, and table use the same conventions as Background and Positive Control QC.
  • Cross-plate overview (per control). x = antigen (panel order), y = MFI (log scale). Once ≥ 3 past plates exist, a grey bar spans the historical IQR (Q1–Q3) of each antigen's per-plate mean (hover the bar for Q1 / median / Q3); this plate's mean is a dot — blue within the IQR, orange ♦ above/below it (provisional flag). With < 3 past plates each past plate's mean is a grey dot and this plate's mean is blue. Hover a current dot to read its individual well MFIs.
  • Per-control stats table (collapsed). One row per antigen: n wells; one column per well (this plate's MFI); current Mean / SD / %CV / IQR across the wells; Historical mean / SD / %CV / IQR across each past plate's mean; and n past plates.
A clean NC programme reads low and stable across plates — a point that jumps up flags cross-reactivity, NC contamination, or a bead-region issue. Deeper NC-level QC (per-antigen thresholds, drift flags) is still in development.

What does "Fit OK" mean? Each antigen's standard points are fit to a 4-parameter logistic (4PL) curve. A fit is marked OK only when all four of these criteria pass:
  • R² ≥ 0.95 on log10(MFI) — the curve actually tracks the data.
  • IC50 inside the tested dilution range (with ±3× margin) — the inflection isn't off the side of the curve.
  • Hill slope between 0.3 and 5.0 — the curve isn't suspiciously flat or step-like.
  • Dynamic range ≥ 3× — upper-plateau MFI is at least 3× the lower-plateau MFI, so there's real signal to fit.
A fit that converges but fails any of these is marked FAIL; the parameters are still shown, but the LLOQ / ULOQ and %-in-range are unreliable.
