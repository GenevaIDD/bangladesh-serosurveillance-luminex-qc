How Background QC works. Background wells are the plate blanks — wells with no specimen (sample name Background0; {{ plate_counts.background }} wells on this plate). For each antigen we summarise the signal across those wells and track it over time:
  • Spread within this plate. The individual Background-well MFIs, their SD, and %CV (SD ÷ mean) show how consistent the blank signal is well-to-well. A high %CV means the antigen's background is noisy on this plate.
  • This plate vs history. The overview below compares this plate's mean Background MFI for each antigen against the spread seen on previous plates, so you can spot antigens reading unusually high or low this run.
A reference max-MFI threshold ({{ bg_max_mfi }}, editable on the Settings page) is tracked, but formal Background pass/fail flagging is still in development — no antigens are flagged as failing here yet.

What does "Fit OK" mean? Each antigen's standard points are fit to a 4-parameter logistic (4PL) curve. A fit is marked OK only when all four of these criteria pass:
  • R² ≥ 0.95 on log10(MFI) — the curve actually tracks the data.
  • IC50 inside the tested dilution range (with ±3× margin) — the inflection isn't off the side of the curve.
  • Hill slope between 0.3 and 5.0 — the curve isn't suspiciously flat or step-like.
  • Dynamic range ≥ 3× — upper-plateau MFI is at least 3× the lower-plateau MFI, so there's real signal to fit.
A fit that converges but fails any of these is marked FAIL; the parameters are still shown, but the LLOQ / ULOQ and %-in-range are unreliable.

Negative Control QC
How Negative Control QC works. A negative control (NC) is a well of pooled seronegative serum (here, pre-2019 North American plasma) that should read antibody-negative for every antigen. The panel plot below shows the mean NC MFI per antigen, tracked across plates; the current plate's point is ringed. Each negative control (e.g. Negative 0 and Negative 49) is its own coloured line — duplicate wells of a control are averaged together, but the two controls are kept separate. A clean NC programme reads low and stable across plates — a point that jumps up flags cross-reactivity, NC contamination, or a bead-region issue.
  • NC wells are matched by the Negative-Control pattern (default Negative, i.e. the Pilot Control: Negative … samples; editable on the Settings page).
  • The panels (a static grid) and the per-well detail below both cover every antigen on the plate.
Note: deeper NC-level QC (per-antigen thresholds, drift flags) is still in development.
